# Writing on Lumina

There are no rules here. Just a few suggestions from people who've found them helpful.

---

**Start in the middle.**
You don't need context or preamble.
Drop the reader into the moment.

---

**Say the specific thing, not the general one.**
Not "I was sad." — "I sat in my car in the parking lot for twenty minutes before I could go inside."

Specificity is what makes a stranger feel understood.

---

**You don't have to resolve it.**
Your story doesn't need a lesson or a happy ending. It's okay to end in the middle of figuring it out. That's usually where the most honest writing lives.

---

**Write for one person, not a crowd.**
Imagine one person who needs to read exactly what you're about to write. Write it for them.

---

**Short is not less than long.**
A single sentence, if it's true, is worth more than five paragraphs that aren't.

---

**Edit once. Then let it go.**
The version you keep polishing forever is usually worse than the one you almost published.
